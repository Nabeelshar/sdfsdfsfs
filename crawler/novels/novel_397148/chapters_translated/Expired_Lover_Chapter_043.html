Chapter 67, Section 43: An Old Friend B City, halfway up a mountain, a villa. Qin Lang lit incense, its light fragrance filling the room. He said to the man before him, "Master Liang, say something." The man, dressed in black casual clothes, sat in a wheelchair, his eyes closed. He was still tall, but much thinner than last year. Qin Lang patted Liang Yiming, but Liang Yiming didn't react at all. "You're really awkward. Let's talk. What are you thinking?" After a while, Liang Yiming finally said, "I'm fine." Qin Lang chuckled inwardly, knowing that Liang Yiming was just putting on a brave face. This year, Liang Yiming's insomnia had worsened to the point where he relied on medication to sleep. He spoke less and less during conversations, and his defensiveness grew stronger. Moreover, this family—the aunt teaching the young lady to climb into bed, the younger brother climbing into the older brother's bed—they were all crazy. "Robles was hurt, don't you have any thoughts?" Qin Lang stood up and walked to Liang Yiming's side. "Your younger brother is now on the same side as his father, the shareholders are like fence-sitters, don't you have any thoughts either? Your people betrayed you like this, don't you feel anything?" Qin Lang looked down and could see the dark circles under Liang Yiming's eyes, the taut jawline revealing sharp lines, making him look somewhat gloomy. "Liang Yiming, what exactly are you thinking?" What was he thinking? Liang Yiming looked up into the darkness, the heavy breathing of Qin Lang filling his ears. He'd never noticed it before in his twenty-odd years; he realized how loud human breathing could be. It was so loud it kept him awake. Not just breathing, but also footsteps, conversation, laughter, even the sound of time tearing at his skin. Liang Yiming, sitting in his wheelchair, slightly raised his head. His lips were flat, his gaze unfocused as he found Qin Lang's location. He slowly spoke, "I don't have any intentions." Qin Lang was enraged by those words. Defense, still defense. He had tried to coax him gently, but Liang Yiming was like a clam shell; he stubbornly refused to tell the truth. He pointed at Liang Yiming, "Liang Yiming, you're such a damn fake." "Not saying anything doesn't mean it's over... I'm your therapist. Keeping your thoughts hidden from me like this won't do your job. Do you understand? I know you can tough it out. Even grass can eventually crush a camel. Dragging it out isn't the solution, do you understand..." Qin Lang's mouth was dry, feeling like he was about to explode with anger. "The result of this is harming others, like Robles..." Finally, Qin Lang saw Liang Yiming's breathing quicken. For a moment, his hand on the wheelchair handle tightened, his head lowered slightly, as if he didn't want anyone to see what was going on, but the veins on his forehead bulged slightly. The atmosphere in the room was deathly silent, as if the air had frozen. Qin Lang took a step forward and suddenly reached out to Liang Yiming. Before he could even reach Liang Yiming, Liang Yiming had already steered his wheelchair back a step. His eyes couldn't see, yet he could still sense and avoid the movement—meaning the other's tense nerves hadn't relaxed for a single moment. Qin Lang withdrew his probing hand. "That Xia Sang from before, didn't you like someone else? I heard that General Manager Wang contacted Xia Sang? How did it go? Did he agree? By the way, did you ask General Manager Wang to contact her…?" Qin Lang relaxed his shoulders and continued. "It's not liking, just a crush," Liang Yiming replied.
